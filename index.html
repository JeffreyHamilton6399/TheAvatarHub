About the Film

Avatar: Aang, The Last Airbender is the first feature film from Avatar Studios — a dedicated division of Nickelodeon founded in 2021 by the franchise's original creators, Bryan Konietzko and Michael DiMartino. Set after the defeat of Fire Lord Ozai, the film continues the story of Team Avatar in their young adulthood, picking up the threads of a world still healing from a century of war.

Directed by Lauren Montgomery — a storyboard artist on the original series and supervising producer of The Legend of Korra — the film blends 2D hand-drawn characters with 3D computer-animated environments via Deep Canvas technology, the same hybrid technique pioneered by Walt Disney Animation in Tarzan and Treasure Planet.

The principal cast was thoughtfully recast to reflect the cultural backgrounds of each nation, with Eric Nam leading as Aang, joined by Jessica Matten, Román Zaragoza, Dionne Quan, and Steven Yeun. Original series composer Jeremy Zuckerman returns with full orchestral support for a score that expands on what was possible in the television series.
